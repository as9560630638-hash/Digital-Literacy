# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## DO's

1. **Keep your profiles professional.** Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and write a bio that reflects your academic and career interests.

2. **Think before you post.** Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parents, or a future employer saw this?" If not, don't post it.

3. **Engage positively and constructively.** Comment and share content that adds value — academic discussions, industry news, project updates, and peer recognition.

4. **Use privacy settings wisely.** Regularly review who can see your posts, stories, and personal details. Keep personal information visible only to trusted connections.

5. **Give credit where it is due.** Always tag or mention the original creator when sharing someone else's content, ideas, or artwork.

6. **Follow and engage with industry professionals.** Connect with professors, alumni, and professionals in your field to build a meaningful network early in your career.

7. **Update your academic and skills sections regularly.** LinkedIn and GitHub profiles should reflect your latest projects, certifications, and achievements.

---

## DON'Ts

1. **Don't share personal information publicly.** Avoid posting your phone number, home address, ID numbers, or financial details on any public platform.

2. **Don't engage in or ignore cyberbullying.** Never participate in trolling, harassment, or negative comment campaigns. Report and block accounts that engage in abusive behaviour.

3. **Don't post or share unverified news.** Spreading misinformation — even unintentionally — can damage your reputation and harm others. Always verify before sharing.

4. **Don't share copyrighted material without permission.** Uploading movies, songs, or textbook scans is illegal and can lead to account suspension or legal consequences.

5. **Don't use social media to vent about college, professors, or classmates.** Negative posts about people or institutions can go viral and have lasting consequences for your academic and professional future.

6. **Don't accept friend/connection requests from strangers without verifying them.** Unknown individuals may attempt phishing, scamming, or social engineering through these connections.

7. **Don't neglect your digital footprint.** Old posts, photos, and comments remain searchable. Periodically review and clean up your past activity.
